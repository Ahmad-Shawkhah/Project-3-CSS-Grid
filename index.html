Glacier

Located in Montana along the Canadian border, Glacier National Park features over 700 miles of trails through pristine forests, alpine meadows, and rugged mountains.

The park is home to grizzly bears, mountain goats, and over 700 plant species. Its iconic Going-to-the-Sun Road crosses the Continental Divide at Logan Pass.

Olympic

Olympic National Park in Washington state protects three distinct ecosystems: glacier-capped mountains, old-growth temperate rainforest, and wild Pacific coastline.

From the Hoh Rain Forest receiving 140 inches of rain per year to the dry rain shadow of Sequim, Olympic offers unmatched ecological diversity in one park.

Death Valley

In this below-sea-level basin, steady drought and record summer heat make Death Valley a land of extremes. Yet, each extreme has a striking contrast. Towering peaks are frosted with winter snow. Rare rainstorms bring vast fields of wildflowers. Winter brings surprisingly mild and inviting weather.

Sequoia

Home to the largest trees on Earth by volume, Sequoia National Park in California protects thousands of ancient giant sequoias. General Sherman, the world's largest tree by volume, stands 275 feet tall and is estimated to be over 2,700 years old. Visitors can hike through groves of these towering giants and experience true natural wonder.
